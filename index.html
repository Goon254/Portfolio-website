My Journey

From Eldama Ravine to the digital frontier - every challenge shaped the builder I am today

I was born and raised in Kaburwa, a small village in Eldama Ravine, where my parents instilled in me the value of education despite many challenges. Running to and from school every day sparked a passion for athletics that shaped my discipline and determination.

I later attended Chewoyet High School in Kapenguria, West Pokot — an environment full of obstacles that I learned to embrace and overcome. During high school, I represented my school in races and competitions, which became some of my most enjoyable and formative moments.

After graduation, my journey led me across the world when I earned a scholarship to study Computer Science at Southern New Hampshire University. This opportunity has been the most transformative milestone of my life — one that not only opened doors, but also allowed me to dream bigger than I ever imagined.

My curiosity for technology started early. Growing up, tech was rare, but whenever I encountered it, I was captivated — whether it was holding a relative's Nokia phone or playing the classic Snake game. That curiosity grew into a passion, and today, I design apps and write programs that once felt out of reach.

I believe this is only the beginning. Every challenge I've faced has prepared me for the future — one I know is filled with even greater opportunities to grow, innovate, and make an impact.
